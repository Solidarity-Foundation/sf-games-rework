There are two characters in this game - Susheela and Imran. The goal of the game is to educate the player about smart ways to handle money through the story arcs or life journeys of the two characters and the monetary choices they need to make under challenging circumstances/scenarios. The characters have goals they want to achieve in their lives in the time span given in the game.

## Susheela's Financial Journey

**Character Profile:**

- Name: Susheela (29 years old, transwoman)
- Background: Working-class family in rural Tamil Nadu, moved to Bangalore at 19 after family rejection
- Profession: Part-time sex worker + beauty services provider (transitioning to full-time beauty parlor)
- Starting Monthly Income: ₹18,000 (informal, irregular)
- Living Situation: Rents small room in Malleswaram with two other trans women
- Community Context: Part of local hijra community under guru Lakshmi, supports younger trans women
- Family: Sends money secretly to supportive mother; has informally adopted daughter Priya (7 years old) from deceased friend
- Health: Diabetic, needs regular medication (₹2,000/month)
- Timeline: 18 years (10 questions, each progressing ~1.8 years)
- Goals: (1) Support Priya's education through college, (2) Buy small house in the outskirts of Bengaluru for retirement by age 50

**Scoring System**

- Excellent Choice: +30 points (demonstrates strong financial literacy)
- Poor Choice: -20 points (common mistake that leads to financial problems)
- Moderate Choice: +10 points (acceptable but not optimal approach)

### Scenario 10:

**Estate Planning and Protecting Priya's Future**
Age: 45.2 | Timeline: 16.2 years | Final phase: 3.8 years to retirement
Susheela has recovered and rebuilt her finances.
Her current position:

- Retirement corpus: ₹16 lakh (growing steadily)
- Business value: ₹8 lakh
- Monthly income: ₹65000
- Priya is graduating, getting job (₹35000/month; contributes ₹20,000/month to household, keeps ₹15,000 for herself)

Susheela realizes she needs estate planning. Priya's adoption was never legally formalized. If something happens to Susheela, her biological family (who rejected her) could legally claim everything. Priya would get nothing.

Her options are:

3. Add Priya as nominee on all accounts (free) - simple but weak legally
4. Create Trust with Priya as beneficiary (₹80,000) - most legally protective but expensive

**(Question) Susheela's Dilemma: How should Susheela protect Priya's inheritance legally?**

₹45000 cost but strongest legal protection for Priya.

**Score: (+10 points)**
**Financial Impact:**
Cheaper option, good protection but adoption gap remains
