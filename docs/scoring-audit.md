# Scoring Audit — Financial Literacy Game
*All 10 scenarios · Point values: +30 excellent, +10 moderate, -20 poor · Max 300*

---

## S1 · The First Stable Income
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +10 | Take extra sex work (+₹5K income, +₹2K expenses → net +₹3K/mo) |
| B | +30 | Renegotiate obligations (−₹1K expenses, sustainable) |
| C | −20 | Maintain all obligations, save nothing (+₹500 expenses) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical.**
B is correct as best: renegotiation is dignified and sustainable. A earns more income but at personal cost ("sacrifice health") and only nets +₹3K/mo after extra expenses — hence moderate. C saves nothing and drains income, correctly penalised.

## S2 · The Police Raid
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Pay bribe + open shop (−₹24K savings, +₹12K income, +₹6K expenses) |
| B | +10 | Pay police only (−₹8K savings, no income change) |
| C | −20 | Fight with lawyer (−₹15K savings, no income change) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical.**
A is the only choice that converts crisis into opportunity (net +₹6K/month, formal business). B is a safe holding pattern. C is the principled choice but financially worst — spending ₹15K with no upside is the weakest financial outcome. The penalty may feel harsh to players who see C as morally right, but from a *financial literacy* perspective it correctly illustrates the cost of fighting without resources.

## S3 · Identity Documents and Banking Access
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Documents + community loan + Priya ₹3K/mo (−₹18,480, +₹1K expenses) |
| B | −20 | Skip documents, help family (−₹6K savings, +₹3K expenses) |
| C | +10 | Documents + reduce Priya's expenses (−₹18K savings, no expense change) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical.**
A gets documents (long-term access to formal credit/banking — essential for all future choices) AND supports Priya adequately. C also gets documents but underinvests in Priya. B skips documents entirely, which closes off formal financial pathways — rightly penalised heavily. The ordering A > C > B is sound.

## S4 · Building Assets
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Buy land (−₹3L savings → land asset ₹3L) |
| B | +10 | Gold + community fund (−₹3L savings → gold ₹2L + community stake ₹1L) |
| C | −20 | Expand business (−₹3.5L savings → equipment ₹2L + gold ₹1L + stake ₹50K, +₹5K income) |

**Assessment: ⚠️ Mostly logical, one concern.**
Land (A) is the strongest choice: it appreciates, enables S6 education funding, and counts toward the house goal. Gold (B) is a decent store of value but less versatile than land — moderate score is fair.

**Concern with C:** Equipment depreciates, and a trans woman's informal business has no property security. But C also adds +₹5K/month income — over 24 months that's +₹1.2L, which is meaningful. The −20 may be slightly harsh for a choice that genuinely increases income. However, C also costs ₹50K *more* than A or B, and the income gain depends on business stability. −20 is defensible but borderline. Consider changing to 0 pts if the intent is to treat it as neutral rather than penalised.

## S5 · Healthcare Crisis and Insurance Barriers
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Emergency fund + cooperative (−₹1L into EF asset, +₹3,250 expenses) |
| B | −20 | Buy private insurance (+₹4,250 expenses, likely denied/excluded) |
| C | +10 | Pay out of pocket (+₹3K expenses, no buffer) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical — very well designed.**
A correctly demonstrates the "emergency fund" concept: locking ₹1L as a dedicated medical corpus is the best risk management for someone without insurance access. B is penalised because private insurance companies exclude trans women — spending ₹15K/year on coverage that doesn't pay out is a financial trap, well-illustrated here. C is a neutral but vulnerable position — adequate for now, but exposed in S9.

## S6 · Priya's Education
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Sell land + cooperative loan (EMI ₹8K/mo, Priya gets B.E. degree) — requires land + surplus |
| B | +10 | Sell land + fund diploma (+₹2.5L savings, Priya gets diploma) — requires land |
| C | −20 | Keep land, personal loan → Priya drops out (spendAllSavings, +₹12.5K EMI) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical, with a systemic note.**
A is clearly best: Priya completes her degree with manageable EMI. B is moderate: debt-free but Priya's education is incomplete. C is worst: highest EMI, all savings gone, and Priya drops out anyway.

**Systemic note:** Players who took S4-B/C (no land) cannot choose A or B — only C is available (and only if surplus ≥ ₹12,500). This is intentional game design (choices have long-term consequences) but facilitators should know that S4 choice essentially determines S6 access. Consider whether players without land should have *any* viable S6 path (e.g., a gold-backed loan option).

## S7 · Business Expansion vs. Stability
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Expand to shopping complex (−₹3L savings, +₹25K income, +₹11K EMI) |
| B | +10 | Stay put, save steadily (no change) |
| C | −20 | Partial expansion at current location (−₹2L savings, +₹10K income, no debt) |

**Assessment: ❌ C's penalty is questionable.**
A is correctly the best: premium location, +₹14K net monthly gain (after EMI), transformative for income. B is conservative stability — moderate is fair.

**Issue with C:** Choice C adds ₹10K/month income with *no new debt* and costs only ₹2L (less than A's ₹3L down payment). Penalising it at −20 makes little financial sense — it's a safer, profitable choice. The intent may be to discourage "half-measures" and push for bold expansion (A) or prudent saving (B), but from a pure financial standpoint C should not score lower than B.

**Recommendation:** Change C from −20 to 0 pts (neutral). The equipment depreciates, but the ₹10K income gain over 24 months (+₹2.4L) more than offsets the ₹2L cost. It shouldn't be penalised.

## S8 · Retirement Corpus Building
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Diversified SIP + PPF + RD (₹25K/mo invested → 3 assets worth ₹6.46L) — requires ₹25K surplus |
| B | +10 | Land + gold + FD (−₹25L savings → land ₹12L + gold ₹5L + FD ₹8L) |
| C | −20 | All savings into RD (spendAllSavings, single instrument) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical.**
A is correctly best: modern diversified portfolio, tax-advantaged (PPF), and growth-oriented. B is the traditional "physical assets" approach — respectable but less diversified than A. C concentrates everything in one instrument and locks up emergency flexibility — rightly penalised.

**Note:** A requires ₹25K/month surplus. Players who don't have this (e.g., those who took S7-B and have lower income) cannot access the best choice. This is a realistic constraint that reinforces the importance of earlier income-building choices.

## S9 · Health Crisis and Emergency Fund Test
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Pay from savings (−₹5L savings) — requires ₹5L savings |
| B | +10 | Sell land (land removed, balance to savings) — requires land |
| C | −20 | Liquidate other assets + 20% loan for shortfall |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical.**
A is best: no assets lost, no debt — this is the exact payoff of building savings. B is moderate: an asset is permanently lost to meet the crisis. C is worst: 20% interest is punishing, and asset liquidation is irreversible. The scoring correctly demonstrates the value of maintaining liquid savings (S5-A's emergency fund pays off here).

**Systemic note:** Players with neither land nor sufficient savings *nor* other assets have no good option and will inevitably take C (or be locked out). This is a realistic hardship but may feel punitive. Facilitators should discuss how S5 emergency fund directly affects S9 outcomes.

## S10 · Estate Planning and Protecting Priya's Future
| Choice | Points | Summary |
|--------|--------|---------|
| A | +30 | Formal adoption + Will (−₹45K, comprehensive legal protection) |
| B | +10 | Will + joint accounts (−₹5K, partial protection) |
| C | −20 | Nominations only (free, legally weakest) |

**Assessment: ✅ Logical — especially important for this community.**
For a trans woman without legal family recognition, formal adoption and a registered will are critical — blood relatives can legally challenge nominations alone. A is clearly best. B provides meaningful but incomplete protection. C leaves Priya's inheritance legally vulnerable. The scoring is well-calibrated for the workshop audience.

## Summary of Issues

| Scenario | Issue | Severity | Recommendation |
|----------|-------|----------|---------------|
| S4-C | −20 for expanding business that adds ₹5K/month income | Minor | Consider changing to 0 pts |
| S7-C | −20 for partial expansion with +₹10K income and NO debt — worse score than doing nothing | **Significant** | Change to 0 pts |
| S6 (gold path) | No land → only C available → guaranteed dropout | Design issue | Consider adding a gold-backed loan path |
| S8-A | Requires ₹25K surplus — locked out for lower-income paths | Constraint | Expected; documents well in workshop framing |
| S9-C | Forced on players with no savings, no land, no assets | Consequence | Expected; links back to S5 emergency fund lesson |

## Overall Verdict

The scoring system is **largely logical and well-designed**, especially for the game's pedagogical goals. The hierarchy of choices (long-term thinking > conservative stability > reactive crisis management) is consistent throughout. The two clearest issues are **S7-C** (penalising a profitable, debt-free choice) and **S4-C** (the income gain makes −20 feel harsh). Everything else is defensible.
